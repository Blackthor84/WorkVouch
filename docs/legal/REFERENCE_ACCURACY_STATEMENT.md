# PeerCV Reference Accuracy Statement

**Last Updated: January 2025**

## 1. Overview

PeerCV facilitates peer references between coworkers to verify work history and provide insights into work quality. This statement explains how references work, accuracy expectations, dispute resolution, and how references affect Trust Scores.

## 2. What Are Peer References?

### 2.1 Definition
- **Peer References** are written evaluations from verified coworkers
- References are based on actual work experience at the same company
- References reflect the opinions and experiences of individual coworkers
- References are not professional background checks or credit reports

### 2.2 Purpose
- Verify that you worked at a specific company
- Provide insights into your work quality and performance
- Help employers evaluate candidates based on peer feedback
- Build your Trust Score through verified references

## 3. How References Are Created

### 3.1 Reference Request Process
1. You add a job to your profile
2. PeerCV matches you with potential coworkers based on company and dates
3. You send coworker verification requests
4. Coworkers confirm they worked with you
5. You request references from verified coworkers
6. Coworkers write references about your work

### 3.2 Verification Requirements
- Coworkers must verify they worked at the same company during overlapping dates
- Only verified coworkers can write references
- References must be based on actual work experience
- References cannot be written by non-coworkers

## 4. Accuracy Expectations

### 4.1 What PeerCV Verifies
- **Employment Verification**: We verify that coworkers worked at the same company during overlapping dates
- **Coworker Identity**: We verify that reference authors are real users with verified accounts
- **Reference Authenticity**: We verify that references are written by verified coworkers

### 4.2 What PeerCV Does NOT Verify
- **Reference Content**: We do not verify the accuracy of reference content
- **Reference Opinions**: We do not verify that reference opinions are fair or accurate
- **Job Performance**: We do not verify claims about job performance or work quality
- **Reference Completeness**: We do not verify that references include all relevant information

### 4.3 Reference Limitations
- References reflect individual opinions, not objective facts
- References may be biased, incomplete, or inaccurate
- References should be considered as one data point among many
- Employers should not rely solely on references for hiring decisions

## 5. Dispute Resolution

### 5.1 Disputing References
- You may dispute references you believe are false, defamatory, or inaccurate
- Disputes must be submitted through PeerCV support
- Disputes will be reviewed within 30 business days
- PeerCV will investigate disputes and take appropriate action

### 5.2 Dispute Criteria
- **False Information**: References containing factually incorrect information
- **Defamation**: References containing defamatory or libelous statements
- **Non-Coworker**: References written by someone who did not work with you
- **Harassment**: References containing harassing or abusive content
- **Violation of Terms**: References that violate PeerCV's Terms of Service

### 5.3 Dispute Outcomes
- **Reference Removal**: False or defamatory references may be removed
- **Reference Correction**: References may be corrected if inaccurate
- **Account Action**: Reference authors may face account suspension or termination
- **No Action**: Disputes that do not meet criteria will be dismissed

### 5.4 Appeal Process
- You may appeal dispute decisions within 30 days
- Appeals will be reviewed by a different PeerCV team member
- Appeal decisions are final

## 6. How References Affect Trust Scores

### 6.1 Trust Score Calculation
- Trust Scores are calculated based on multiple factors, including references
- References contribute to your Trust Score based on:
  - Number of references received
  - Quality/rating of references
  - Verification status of reference authors
  - Recency of references

### 6.2 Reference Impact
- **Positive References**: High-quality, positive references increase Trust Score
- **Negative References**: Negative references may decrease Trust Score
- **Reference Quantity**: More references generally increase Trust Score
- **Reference Recency**: Recent references may have more impact than old references

### 6.3 Trust Score Updates
- Trust Scores are recalculated nightly
- New references are included in the next recalculation
- Removed references are excluded from recalculation
- Trust Score changes are reflected within 24 hours

## 7. Reference Quality Standards

### 7.1 What Makes a Good Reference
- **Specific**: References should include specific examples and details
- **Relevant**: References should be relevant to the job and work performed
- **Honest**: References should be honest and accurate
- **Professional**: References should be professional and respectful

### 7.2 What Makes a Bad Reference
- **Vague**: References that are too general or lack detail
- **Inaccurate**: References containing false or misleading information
- **Defamatory**: References containing defamatory or libelous statements
- **Unprofessional**: References containing unprofessional or inappropriate content

## 8. Employer Use of References

### 8.1 Recommended Use
- Use references as one factor among many in candidate evaluation
- Consider references in context with interviews, skills assessments, and other data
- Verify reference claims through additional sources when possible
- Do not rely solely on references for hiring decisions

### 8.2 Limitations
- References are opinions, not guarantees of future performance
- References may be biased or incomplete
- References should not be used to discriminate against protected classes
- References should not be the sole basis for adverse employment decisions

### 8.3 Legal Compliance
- Employers must comply with all applicable hiring laws
- References should not be used in violation of FCRA, EEOC, or local employment laws
- Employers should consult legal counsel for compliance questions

## 9. Reference Privacy

### 9.1 Who Can See References
- **You**: You can see all references written about you
- **Employers**: Employers with active subscriptions can see references (with your consent)
- **Reference Authors**: Authors can see references they wrote
- **Public**: References are not publicly visible (only to you and authorized employers)

### 9.2 Reference Anonymization
- References may be anonymized if the author's account is deleted
- Author names may be removed while reference content remains
- Anonymized references may still be visible to you and employers

## 10. Best Practices

### 10.1 For Reference Recipients
- Request references from multiple coworkers when possible
- Consider references in context with other information
- Dispute false or defamatory references
- Build a diverse portfolio of references

### 10.2 For Reference Authors
- Write honest, accurate, and professional references
- Include specific examples and details
- Focus on work-related qualities and performance
- Avoid defamatory or unprofessional content

### 10.3 For Employers
- Use references as one data point among many
- Verify reference claims through additional sources
- Consider references in context with other evaluation methods
- Comply with all applicable hiring laws
