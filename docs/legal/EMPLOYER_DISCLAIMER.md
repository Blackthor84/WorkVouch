# Employer Disclaimer and Guidelines

**Last Updated: January 2025**

## Important Legal Notice for Employers

PeerCV provides verification and reference services to help employers evaluate candidates. However, employers must understand and comply with the following legal requirements and limitations.

## 1. Trust Score Limitations

### 1.1 Not for FCRA Decisions
- **PeerCV Trust Scores are NOT consumer reports under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)**
- Trust Scores should NOT be used as the sole basis for adverse employment decisions
- Trust Scores are peer-verified indicators, not credit or background checks
- Using Trust Scores for FCRA-covered decisions may violate federal law

### 1.2 What Trust Scores Represent
- Trust Scores reflect peer verification and reference quality
- Scores are calculated based on verified employment and coworker references
- Scores are indicators of verification completeness, not job performance predictions
- Trust Scores do not guarantee future job performance

### 1.3 Recommended Use
- Use Trust Scores as one factor among many in candidate evaluation
- Combine Trust Scores with interviews, skills assessments, and other evaluation methods
- Consider Trust Scores as verification of work history, not performance predictions

## 2. Non-Discrimination Requirements

### 2.1 Equal Employment Opportunity
- Employers must comply with all applicable equal employment opportunity laws
- Do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or other protected characteristics
- Peer references and opinions must not be used to discriminate against protected classes

### 2.2 Peer Opinion Limitations
- Peer references reflect individual opinions, not objective facts
- Do not use negative peer opinions as the sole basis for hiring decisions
- Consider the context and relevance of peer references
- Be aware of potential bias in peer references

### 2.3 Best Practices
- Use peer references to verify work history and dates
- Consider peer references as one data point in a comprehensive evaluation
- Do not make assumptions based on single references
- Seek multiple references when possible

## 3. Compliance with Local Hiring Laws

### 3.1 Jurisdiction-Specific Requirements
- Employers must comply with all applicable federal, state, and local hiring laws
- Laws vary by jurisdiction and may include:
  - Ban-the-box laws (restricting criminal history inquiries)
  - Salary history bans
  - Fair chance hiring laws
  - State-specific background check requirements

### 3.2 Legal Consultation
- PeerCV is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice
- Employers should consult with legal counsel to ensure compliance
- Employers are solely responsible for compliance with applicable laws

### 3.3 Prohibited Uses
- Do not use PeerCV data to violate any applicable laws
- Do not use PeerCV data for discriminatory purposes
- Do not use PeerCV data in violation of employment contracts or agreements

## 4. User Privacy and Consent

### 4.1 Respecting User Privacy
- Employers must respect candidate privacy rights
- Do not share candidate profiles or references outside your organization without consent
- Do not use candidate data for purposes other than hiring evaluation
- Comply with data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

### 4.2 Consent Requirements
- Candidates must consent to employer access to their profiles
- Employers may only view profiles of candidates who have opted in
- Employers must not access profiles without proper authorization

### 4.3 Data Security
- Employers are responsible for securing candidate data
- Do not store or share candidate profiles insecurely
- Implement appropriate access controls and data protection measures

## 5. Accuracy and Verification

### 5.1 Verification Status
- PeerCV verifies employment through coworker matching
- Verification confirms that coworkers worked at the same company during overlapping dates
- Verification does not guarantee the accuracy of job titles, responsibilities, or performance

### 5.2 Reference Accuracy
- Peer references reflect the opinions of individual coworkers
- PeerCV does not verify the accuracy of reference content
- Employers should consider references as one data point among many

### 5.3 Dispute Resolution
- Candidates may dispute references or Trust Scores
- Employers should consider disputes when evaluating candidates
- PeerCV will investigate disputes and update information as appropriate
